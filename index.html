Guidelines

Scratch (High School)


Scratch is an educational programming language and multimedia tool that can be used by pupils, teachers and parents for a range of educational and entertainment projects from maths and science projects, including simulations and visualisations of experiments, recording lectures with animated presentations, to social sciences animated stories, and interactive art and music. Simple games can be made with it, as well.

Alice 3 (PUC)


Alice is an innovative 3D programming environment that makes it easy to create an animation for telling story, playing an interactive game, or a video to share on the web. Alice is a teaching tool for introductory computing. It uses 3D graphics and drag-and-drop interface to facilitate a more engaging, less frustrating first programming experience.

Rules and Regulations:


  • 2 students make a team in both the competitions.
  • Any number of teams can participate from school/college.
  • Theme will be given on the spot, participants have to develop simulations/games based on the theme provided. Time duration for the competition is 2 hours.
  • Any materials(pen drives/portable devices) other than that provided by the organisers should not be used. Marks will be based on creativity and quality of the final solution generated.
  • Judges decision will be full and final.
  • CDs containing softwares, instructions and tutorials will be provided to the participants.
  • Refreshments, lunch and Transportation will be provided.
